Integrating Life Cycle Analyses and Circular Economy: A Pathway Towards Sustainable Development in Bangladesh

Mahreen Mustafa

DOI Google Scholar
This paper examines how integrating Life Cycle Analyses (LCA) and Circular Economy (CE) frameworks can address environmental and social challenges in Bangladesh's textile sector. The study identifies tangible benefits including reductions in water and energy consumption, lower carbon emissions, and advancements in waste recycling — all contributing to Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). It explores the interconnections among LCA, CE, and the SDGs, synthesizing existing literature and empirical data, and examines diverse perspectives from key institutions in Bangladesh. The paper advocates for policy interventions, capacity building, and stakeholder engagement to propel Bangladesh towards a more resource-efficient and socially inclusive future.
2020 Springer Nature

Community-Based Socioeconomic Development Programs: A Catalyst for SDGs

Shahana Afrose Chowdhury, Mahreen Mustafa, Meherun Ahmed, Nushrat Jahan

DOI Google Scholar
Community-based socioeconomic development programs initiate a process that enables Non-Government Organizations (NGOs) to develop sustainable, long-term programs involving community participation for alleviating poverty — acting as a catalyst to growth and development and meeting the Sustainable Development Goals of the United Nations. These programs broadly aim to improve the livelihoods and living conditions of poor rural communities, with emphasis on women and other vulnerable groups.
